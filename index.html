Time

Time For date numbers and hours, use ordinal numbers For minutes, use cardinal numbers Ordinal - tells order Cardinal - tells quantity Change ordinal number from “us” to “a” You can also use fractions to tell 5 minute intervals Hora _(hour)_ et _(minute)_ minutae est - It is _ Quota hora _(activity)? - What time do you _ Use “tu” conjugations when asking _ (activity) hora _(time) - I _ at _

Prepositions

ACCUSATIVE CASE post, + acc., after, behind ad, + acc., to, toward, at prope, + acc., near per, + acc., through in, + acc., into, against trans, + acc., across contra, + acc., against super, + acc., over supra, + acc., above propter, + acc., on account of, because of intra, + acc., within infra, + acc., below inter, + acc., between iuxta, + acc., next to extra, + acc., outside circum, + acc., around ultra, + acc., beyond versus, + acc., toward, against praeter, + acc., beyond, past ABLATIVE CASE sub, + abl., under, beneath in, + abl., in, on, among dë, + abl., down from, concerning, about, sine, + abl., without prö, + abl., on behalf of ä or ab, + abl., away from, by cum, + abl., with ë or ex, + abl., out of, from With words both in ablative and accusative… Accusative – deals with movement – He jumps in the water – movement – I’m walking into my house Ablative – deals with stationary or no movement – He’s in the water – no movement – I walk in my house Cum – with – use cum when talking about people Ablative – works with things Don’t include “cum” when talking about things. Include “cum” when talking about a person.
